The First World War

In June 1914, the heir to the Austrian throne, Franz Ferdinand was murdered at Sarajevo, Serbia, during an official state visit. And after this murder, Austria and Germany declared war on Serbia. When Germany invaded Belgium, Great Britain entered the war as an ally to France and Russia. Soon, half of the nations of the world became involved in the conflict, as well as the colonies of the British Empire.

The Rise of Dictatorships

Dictatorships emerged during the 1920s and 1930s. Totalitarian governments found fertle ground for growth in Russia, Italy, Germany and other countries. In Russia, Communism came to power in the revolution of 1917. In Italy, Mussolini founded the Fascist party in 1919. He managed to quickly take control over the government and transformed Italy into a totalitarian state. In Germany, Nazi came to power in 1933. Adolf Hitler crushed all political opposition and took over the country.

The Horrors of the German Concentration Camps

After the Nazis came to power in 1933, they used concentration camps to imprison political enemies. When the Second World War began, the Nazis decided to rid Europe of all Jews. By the war's end, they had murdered about 6 million Jews in the concentration camps. When allied forces reached the camps in 1945, they found thousands of unburied dead bodies and some survivors. After the war, many Nazi leaders were convicted of crimes against humanity and were executed.

The End of an Age and Social Changes

The death of Queen Victoria, who had been a symbol of the values, the greatness and the weaknesses of the age, marked a turning point. On the Queen's death, her son Edward VII came to the throne. His age can be seen as a period of many social changes. Important milestones in this process were the Education Act, which guaranteded secondary education. Old age pensions and National health Insurance were introduced; and goverment offices, where people went to find jobs, were opened.

The British Commonwealth of Nations

The British Commonwealth of Nations began to take form in the early 20th century when the self-governing countries within the british Empire came toghether for Imperial Conferences. In 1926, representatives of the principal parts of the Empire (South Africa, Canada, Australia, New Zealand and Newfoundland) defined the Commonwealth nations 'autonomous' but still 'united by a common allegiance to the Crown'.

The Second World War

The three main causes of the Second World War were: the problems left unsolved by the First World War; the rise of dictatorships and the desire of germany, Italy and Japan for more territory. The war saw the Allied powers (France and Britain, and later Russia and the USA) agaist the Axis powers (Germany, Italy and Japan). In England the Conservative government was replaced by a coalition government headed by Winston Churchill. His inspired leadership united the people of Britain against Nazi Germany and its allies. He negotiated the alliance with The Soviet Union and the USA and met Stalin and Roosvelt at three major conferences.
